Delicious carbonara recipe

Experience the authentic taste of Rome with this timeless Roman Carbonara recipe, a masterclass in culinary simplicity. This legendary pasta dish relies on the magic of emulsion to create a rich, velvety sauce without using a single drop of cream. Hot, freshly boiled spaghetti is tossed with crisp, savory pork and a velvety mixture of whisked eggs and sharp cheese. The residual heat of the pasta cooks the eggs gently, creating a smooth, glossy coat that clings perfectly to every strand.

Elevate your cooking technique by mastering the balance of traditional, high-quality ingredients. Authentic guanciale provides a deeply savory, fatty base, while a heavy hand of freshly cracked black pepper cuts through the richness with a vibrant, aromatic bite. A generous blending of sharp Pecorino Romano cheese infuses the sauce with a distinctively salty, bold depth. This rapid, single-pan technique transforms basic pantry elements into a sophisticated, restaurant-grade dinner in under twenty minutes.

ingredients:

  • Pasta:1 pound high-quality spaghetti or rigatoni
  • Pork:½ pound guanciale or thick-cut pancetta, cubed
  • Eggs: 4 large egg yolks plus 1 whole large egg
  • Cheese:1 cup freshly grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • Spice:1 tablespoon whole black peppercorns, coarsely crushed
  • Water:1 tablespoon whole black peppercorns, coarsely crushed

Steps:

  1. Cook the spaghetti in salted water until al dente.
  2. Render cubed guanciale in a skillet until golden crisp.
  3. Remove skillet from heat to prevent scrambling the eggs.
  4. Blend egg yolks, whole egg, and Pecorino Romano together.
  5. Stir the crushed black pepper into the warm guanciale.
  6. Reserve one cup of starchy pasta water before draining.
  7. Toss the hot, drained pasta into the skillet.
  8. Add the egg and cheese mixture to the skillet.
  9. Splash in reserved pasta water while tossing vigorously.
  10. Stir rapidly until a smooth, creamy sauce forms.
  11. Plate immediately with extra cheese and black pepper.
